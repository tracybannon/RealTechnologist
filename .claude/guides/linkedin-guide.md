### 3. Educational/Rally Cry (300-400 words)
**When to use**: Adapted blog content, comprehensive guidance, call to action
**Goal**: Educate thoroughly, provide resources, rally community

**Structure**:
1. Rally cry opening ("hashtag#NoHobbyists...")
2. Rhetorical questions setting up problem
3. Short context paragraphs (2-3 sentences each)
4. Transition: "Here are ideas/techniques from my experience and peers:"
5. List with ++ markers
6. Each ++ has unicode bold header + explanation
7. Personal experience woven throughout
8. Counter-intuitive framing when appropriate
9. Credit multiple people
10. Link to resources inline
11. Signature closing: "If you take away a single thought..."
12. 5-7 hashtags including specialty tags

**Template**:
```
hashtag#RallyCry when it comes to [topic]! We cannot allow [problem]!
[Rhetorical question challenging status quo]?

[Personal practice]. [Resource/tool mention with link]
```

**Key elements**:

**Hashtags Inline**:
- Written out: "hashtag#NoHobbyists", "hashtag#BurnTheShips"
- Purpose: Creates emphasis, shows it's a movement/concept
- Use when: Rally cry, repeated mantra, signature theme

### List Formatting

**Emoji Numbers** (1ï¸âƒ£ 2ï¸âƒ£ 3ï¸âƒ£):
- Use for: Short focused lists (Pattern 1)
- Typically: 4-6 items
- Each item: 1-2 sentences

**Plus Markers** (++):
- Use for: Educational lists (Patterns 3, 4, 5)
- Typically: 4-7 items
- Each item: Can be multi-paragraph

**Rocket Emoji Headers** (ğŸš€):
- Use for: Category sections (Pattern 2)
- Shows: Energy, forward momentum
- Typically: 2-3 sections per post

**Regular Bullets** (-):
- Use for: Sub-points under emoji/bold headers
- Keep: Concise, parallel structure

### Writing Style

**Use & Instead of "and"**:
- Saves characters
- More conversational
- Example: "requirements & goals" not "requirements and goals"

**Double Exclamation Marks!!**:
- For strong emphasis
- Typically at end of key statements
- Don't overuse (1-2 per post)

**Parenthetical Asides**:
- Add personality: "(nothing beats OTJ with a safety net)"
- Provide examples: "(e.g., C4 Modeling, UML, SysML)"
- Show reaction: "(YIKES!)"

**Shorthand**:
- "next gen" not "next generation"
- "OTJ" for "on the job"
- "favs" for "favorites"
- Natural, conversational

## Crediting & Tagging

### How to Credit People

**First Reference - Full Name**:
- "Brian Chambers and I have been discussing..."
- "Gary Gruver's EDT course..."
- "Bryan Finster and I focus on this daily"

**Possessive Form**:
- Use: "Patty Azzarello's book MOVE"
- Not: "Patty Azzarello - MOVE"

**Thank Recommenders**:
- "Thanks to Christina Rhylander for the recommendation!"
- Shows grace and credits sources

**Multiple People**:
- Name drop generously
- Shows you're part of community
- Builds relationships

### Linking to Resources

**Inline Links** (not comments):
- Include URLs in post body
- Contrary to algorithm advice
- You prioritize value over reach
- Format: "See [Name]'s [work] [link]"

**Resource Types**:
- Tools: "OWASP training projects like WebGoat [link]"
- Articles: "Synopsys has a quick post on [topic]: [link]"
- Frameworks: "NIST framework [link]"
- Thought leadership: "Lisa Plaggemier's Security Optimism 2022 [link]"

## Hashtag Strategy

### Number of Hashtags by Pattern
- **Pattern 1 (Short)**: 4-5 hashtags
- **Pattern 2 (Stance)**: 4-6 hashtags
- **Pattern 3 (Rally)**: 5-7 hashtags
- **Pattern 4 (Empathetic)**: 8-13 hashtags
- **Pattern 5 (Reflective)**: 8-10 hashtags

**Rule**: Broader/more comprehensive topic = more hashtags

### Always Include
- **#HumansFirst**: When topic relates to people, culture, grace
- **#SomethingToNoodleOn**: When using signature closing
- **#SoftwareArchitecture**: For architecture-related posts
- **#DevSecOps** or **#DevOps**: For modern practices posts

### Specialty Tags
- **#ST4G**: Straight Talk for Government community
- **#CommunityofCommunities**: Community building posts
- **#ADR** or **#ArchitecturalDecisionRecords**: When discussing ADRs
- **#ThreatModeling**, **#CyberSecurity**: Security-focused posts

### Tag Format
- Lowercase for multi-word: #softwarearchitecture
- Or CamelCase: #SoftwareArchitecture
- Be consistent within a post
- Mix is okay across different posts

## Engagement Best Practices

### When to Ask Questions
- **Empathetic posts**: "I'd love to hear how..."
- **Reflective posts**: "What other ways can you think of..."
- **NOT on**: Stance posts, educational posts
- **Why**: You're teaching/leading, not fishing for engagement

### When to Take Stances
- **Counter-trend**: "While there are voices saying X, I believe Y"
- **Rally cry**: "We cannot allow..."
- **Declarative**: "There is no single approach"
- **Why**: Establishes thought leadership

### Personal Experience Always
Every post should include:
- "I'm doing this now..."
- "I advocate..."
- "I cannot overstate..."
- "I'm passionate that..."
- "I can't seem to shake..."

**Why**: Grounds advice in real-world practice

## Pattern Selection Guide

**Choose Pattern 1 (Short & Focused)** when:
- Single concept to teach
- Quick reminder of principle
- Reinforcing signature theme
- Time-sensitive/timely post

**Choose Pattern 2 (Medium with Stance)** when:
- Collaborating with someone (credit them!)
- Counter prevailing trend
- Share framework/categorization
- Take clear position

**Choose Pattern 3 (Educational/Rally)** when:
- Adapting blog post for LinkedIn
- Comprehensive guidance needed
- Multiple resources to share
- Call to action/movement building

**Choose Pattern 4 (Empathetic/Analogical)** when:
- Addressing burnout/exhaustion
- Advocating for grace
- Using extended metaphor
- Softer, compassionate tone needed

**Choose Pattern 5 (Reflective/Philosophical)** when:
- Sharing powerful quote/concept
- Telling historical story
- Applying philosophy to modern practice
- Using "Something to noodle on"
