# Blog Writing Guide

Your blog is where you dive deep into topics, tell comprehensive stories, and provide thorough guidance. Blog posts are longer-form content (800-1500 words) that educate and provoke thought.

---

## Blog Post Structure

### 1. Engaging Hook
Start with something that grabs attention:
- **Question to reader**: "Is Platform Engineering the new DevOps?"
- **Personal address**: "Hello friends, it's been a long time..."
- **Bold statement**: "For heaven's sake, Write It Down!!!"
- **Context setting**: "When it comes to cybersecurity, we have a talent and knowledge deficit..."

**Purpose**: Pull readers in, make them want to keep reading

### 2. Context Setting
Why this matters now:
- What's happening in the industry
- Personal experience that led to writing
- Problem you're seeing repeatedly
- Gap you're addressing

**Length**: 1-3 paragraphs
**Tone**: Conversational, sets stage for what's coming

### 3. Core Content
Break into clear sections with unicode bold headers:

```markdown

[Explanation with examples]

## ğŸ® - [Second Major Point]
[Explanation with examples]
```

**Each section should**:
- Have clear unicode numbered header (ğŸ­, ğŸ®, ğŸ¯...)
- Explain one major concept/point
- Include real-world examples
- Be digestible (3-5 paragraphs)
- Build on previous sections

**Use**:
- Numbered lists for steps/phases
- Bold for key terms on first use
- Italics for emphasis or terms being introduced
- Code blocks for technical content
- Images to break up text

### 4. Actionable Takeaways
What readers should do:
- Specific actions they can take
- Resources to explore
- Frameworks to try
- Questions to ask themselves

**Format**: Often bulleted or numbered list
**Tone**: Practical, enabling

### 5. Call to Engagement
End with invitation:
- "Something to noodle on"
- "You game?"
- Question that calls back to opening
- Forward-looking statement
- "If you take away a single thought..."

### 6. Hashtags
**Count**: 5-7 relevant hashtags
**Format**: `#SomethingToNoodleOn #SoftwareArchitecture #DevSecOps`
**Always include**: #SomethingToNoodleOn when appropriate

### 7. Image Attribution
**Always credit**:
- "Photo by [Name] on Unsplash"
- "Pix thanks to DALL-E"
- "Image by [Name] on [Source]"

### Emphasis

**Bold** for:
- Key terms on first use
- Section titles inline: "The **#NoHobbyists** movement..."
- Strong emphasis: "There should be **#NoHobbyists** when it comes to..."

**Italics** for:
- Terms being introduced: *framework*, *platform*, *architecture*
- Subtle emphasis
- Internal thoughts: "I may sound like a broken record..."

**Unicode Bold** for:
- Major headers only
- Don't overuse in body text
- Saves for structure

### Lists

**When to Number**:
- Steps in a process
- Ordered priorities
- Chronological items
- "Here are X predictions/principles/steps"

**When to Bullet**:
- Unordered items
- Examples
- Related but not sequential
- Sub-points under numbered items

## Voice & Tone for Blogs

### Direct and Warm
```
Hello friends, it's been a long time...
```
- Address readers as "friends", "gang", "folks", "ya'll"
- Conversational, like talking over coffee

### Honest and Personal
```
Being honest, I have to admit I really love tech! It's my passion, my
hobby, and my career.
```
- Share personal experiences
- Admit challenges and learning
- "Being honest...", "The truth is...", "Let's face it..."

### Enthusiastic
```
I am over the moon excited at what's to come.
```
- Show genuine excitement
- "Shazam!!", "game changing", "wonderfully helpful"
- Exclamation marks for emphasis!

### Balanced and Nuanced
```
The idea is NOT to ignore history or context. The idea is to force new
muscle strength and retrain old muscle memory.
```
- Acknowledge complexity
- "Of course...", "That said...", "The crux here is..."
- Avoid oversimplification

## Content Development

### Explaining Concepts

**Start with Why**:
- Why does this matter?
- What problem does it solve?
- Why now?

**Use Analogies**:
```
To understand why we are exhausted, let's use the analogy of learning
to swim...
```
- Relate technical concepts to everyday experiences
- Extend analogies throughout section if helpful

**Build Progressively**:
- Simple explanation first
- Add complexity gradually
- Provide examples at each level

**Show Real Examples**:
- Not just abstract theory
- "I'm doing this now with stream-aligned teams..."
- "In my role as a chief architect..."

### Handling Complexity

**Don't Oversimplify**:
- Acknowledge when topics are complex
- "There is no one-size-fits-all solution"
- "Context matters"
- "Your mileage may vary"

**Make Accessible**:
- Break complex topics into digestible pieces
- Use headers and lists for scanability
- Provide entry points for different knowledge levels

**Acknowledge Nuance**:
```
While there are plenty of voices saying "architecture is dead", I believe
there is a more practical middle ground.
```
- Counter prevailing narratives when appropriate
- Show multiple perspectives
- Take clear stance while acknowledging alternatives

## Blog Length Guidelines

### Standard Post: 800-1200 words
**When**:
- Single focused topic
- Clear message
- 3-5 main points

**Example**: Platform Engineering vs DevOps (~800 words)

### Deep Dive: 1200-1500 words
**When**:
- Complex topic requiring detail
- Multiple related concepts
- Comprehensive guidance with examples

**Example**: #NoHobbyists (~1400 words)

### Series Consideration
When topic exceeds 1500 words:
- Consider breaking into series
- Reference "My hope is to dive into each topic a bit deeper..."
- Link between posts

## Common Blog Patterns

### Prediction/Trend Analysis
**Structure**:
1. Opening: "Hello friends" + context
2. Hook question: "Are you tired of hearing about X?"
3. Numbered predictions (## ğŸ­ - , ## ğŸ® - )
4. Each prediction: explanation + implications
5. Synthesis: How they connect
6. Forward-looking close

**Example**: 2024 AI Predictions

### Myth-Busting
**Structure**:
1. Question opening: "Is X the new Y?"
2. Answer: "Not exactly, no."
3. Explain what it actually is
4. Numbered similarities and differences
5. Practical guidance for choosing
6. Context reminder

**Example**: Platform Engineering vs DevOps

### Advocacy/Rally Cry
**Structure**:
1. Bold statement of problem
2. Why it matters
3. Current state (what's wrong)
4. Numbered recommendations (### headers)
5. Each recommendation: what + how + example
6. Takeaway: "If you take away a single thought..."

**Example**: #NoHobbyists

### Framework/How-To
**Structure**:
1. Introduce framework/concept
2. Why it's valuable
3. Numbered steps/components
4. Each step: explanation + example + tips
5. Common pitfalls
6. Getting started advice

**Example**: Threat Modeling as Team Sport

Before publishing:

**Content**
- [ ] Clear main message/thesis?

- [ ] Real-world examples included?
- [ ] Actionable takeaways provided?

**Structure**
- [ ] Engaging hook?
- [ ] Clear section headers (unicode numbered)?
- [ ] Logical flow between sections?
- [ ] Strong closing?

**Voice**
- [ ] Sounds conversational?
- [ ] Personal experience shared?
- [ ] Appropriate enthusiasm?
- [ ] Signature phrases included naturally?

**Technical**
- [ ] Acronyms explained on first use?
- [ ] Sources cited/linked?
- [ ] People credited?
- [ ] Images attributed?

**Format**
- [ ] Front matter complete?
- [ ] Header image specified?
- [ ] Hashtags included (5-7)?
- [ ] Proper markdown formatting?
- [ ] Links work?

**Length**
- [ ] 800-1500 words?
- [ ] Could be broken into series if longer?
- [ ] Not too short for topic depth?
