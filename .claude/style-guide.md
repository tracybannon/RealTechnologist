## Voice & Tone

### Core Voice Characteristics

- **Direct and Conversational**: Write like you're talking with a colleague over coffee
- **Warm and Approachable**: Be friendly, authentic, and human
- **Enthusiastic**: Show genuine excitement about technology and ideas
- **Honest and Transparent**: Share real experiences, including challenges
- **Experience-Driven**: Ground insights in real-world stories and practical application
- **Community-Focused**: Emphasize connection, collaboration, and shared learning

### Tone Variations by Context

- **Educational**: Patient, thorough, encouraging
- **Thought Leadership**: Confident, informed, nuanced
- **Advocacy**: Passionate, urgent, rallying
- **Critical/Myth-Busting**: Direct, evidence-based, but not harsh

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing on any platform:

**Voice**
- [ ] Does this sound like me talking?
- [ ] Am I being authentic and honest?
- [ ] Have I removed corporate speak?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the platform?

**Value**
- [ ] What's the clear takeaway?
- [ ] Is this actionable or thought-provoking?
- [ ] Have I shared real experience or insight?
- [ ] Will readers be glad they spent time on this?

**Content**
- [ ] Have I explained acronyms and jargon?
- [ ] Are my facts correct and sources cited?
- [ ] Have I credited people mentioned?
- [ ] Am I representing others' ideas fairly?

**Platform-Specific**
- [ ] Right format for platform (unicode, lists, hashtags)?
- [ ] Appropriate length?
- [ ] Hashtags correct count and type?
- [ ] Links work and add value?

**Accessibility**
- [ ] Is the formatting readable?
- [ ] Are images described/credited?
- [ ] Is the language inclusive?
- [ ] Can this be understood by non-native English speakers?

## Common Patterns Across Platforms

- Question: "Is X the new Y?"

- Bold statement: "**Problem**. The root cause will surprise you:"

- "Here's the wrinkle:"

- "Bottom line:"

### Introducing Lists
- "Here's what I'm seeing:"

## Technical Writing Tips

### Explaining Concepts
1. Start with the "why" (context before detail)
2. Use analogies (relate new to familiar)
3. Build progressively (simple to complex)
4. Show real examples (abstract plus concrete)
5. Acknowledge complexity (don't oversimplify)

### Acronyms & Jargon
- Spell out on first use with acronym in parentheses
- Explain when needed (don't assume everyone knows)
- Challenge jargon (ask "is there a clearer way?")
- Provide context clues
- Link to resources for deep dives

### Citations & Credits
- Link to sources generously
- Credit people by full name
- Use "Thanks to [Name]" for influences
- Always credit image sources
- Use hyperlinks not ugly URLs
