# Tracy "Trac" Bannon Style Guide

## Voice & Tone

### Core Voice Characteristics
- **Direct and Conversational**: Write like you're talking with a colleague over coffee
- **Warm and Approachable**: Be friendly, authentic, and human
- **Enthusiastic**: Show genuine excitement about technology and ideas
- **Honest and Transparent**: Share real experiences, including challenges
- **Experience-Driven**: Ground insights in real-world stories and practical application
- **Community-Focused**: Emphasize connection, collaboration, and shared learning

### Tone Variations by Context
- **Educational**: Patient, thorough, encouraging
- **Thought Leadership**: Confident, informed, nuanced
- **Advocacy**: Passionate, urgent, rallying
- **Critical/Myth-Busting**: Direct, evidence-based, but not harsh

## Writing Style

### Sentence Structure
- Mix short, punchy sentences with longer explanatory ones
- Use fragments for emphasis ("Not exactly, no.")
- Start sentences with conjunctions for conversational flow ("And just for fun...")
- Break grammar rules intentionally for impact

### Paragraph Structure
- Keep paragraphs focused and digestible
- Use single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis
- Build momentum with numbered or bulleted lists
- End sections with actionable takeaways

## Technical Writing Guidelines

### Explaining Concepts
1. **Start with the "why"**: Context before detail
2. **Use analogies**: Relate new concepts to familiar ones
3. **Build progressively**: Simple to complex
4. **Show real examples**: Abstract plus concrete
5. **Acknowledge complexity**: Don't oversimplify, but make accessible

### Acronyms & Jargon
- **First use**: Spell out with acronym in parentheses
- **Explain when needed**: Don't assume everyone knows
- **Challenge jargon**: Ask "is there a clearer way to say this?"
- **Context clues**: Help readers infer meaning
- **Link to resources**: When deep dives are available

### Citations & Credits
- Link to sources generously
- Credit people by full name and link to their LinkedIn
- "Thanks to [Name]" for ideas that influenced you
- Credit image sources always
- Use hyperlinks rather than ugly URLs in text

### Data & Statistics
- Cite sources for claims
- Provide context for numbers
- Use comparisons to make data meaningful
- Don't overwhelm with too many stats
- Lead with the insight, not the number

## Values Reflected in Writing

### Inclusivity
- Avoid gendered language (use "they/them" for singular)
- Don't assume knowledge level
- Welcome questions and different perspectives
- Celebrate diverse backgrounds and paths into tech
- Call out when you're simplifying for accessibility

### Growth Mindset
- Frame failures as learning opportunities
- Celebrate experimentation
- Acknowledge what you don't know
- Share your own learning journey
- Encourage trying new approaches

### Practical Over Perfect
- Done is better than perfect
- Start small, iterate
- Share works-in-progress
- Acknowledge constraints
- Real-world application beats theoretical purity

### Empathy & Grace
- Recognize people are doing their best
- Acknowledge systemic challenges
- Don't blame individuals for structural problems
- Extend psychological safety through words
- Remember the human on the other side

## Quality Checklist

### Before Publishing, Ask:

**Clarity**
- [ ] Would someone new to this topic understand?
- [ ] Have I explained acronyms and jargon?
- [ ] Are my examples concrete and relatable?
- [ ] Is the structure easy to follow?

**Voice**
- [ ] Does this sound like me talking?
- [ ] Am I being authentic and honest?
- [ ] Have I removed corporate speak?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the platform?

**Value**
- [ ] What's the takeaway?
- [ ] Is this actionable or thought-provoking?
- [ ] Have I shared real experience or insight?
- [ ] Will readers be glad they spent time on this?

**Engagement**
- [ ] Did I ask questions or invite discussion?
- [ ] Are there places to engage (comments, tags)?
- [ ] Have I made it easy to share?
- [ ] Did I credit/tag relevant people?

**Technical Accuracy**
- [ ] Are my facts correct?
- [ ] Have I cited sources?
- [ ] Am I representing others' ideas fairly?
- [ ] Is my advice sound and considered?

**Accessibility**
- [ ] Is the formatting readable?
- [ ] Are images described/credited?
- [ ] Is the language inclusive?
- [ ] Can this be understood by non-native English speakers?
