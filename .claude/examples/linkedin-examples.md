## Pattern 3: Educational/Rally Cry (~300-400 words)

Adapted from blog content, lots of resources, credits multiple people.

### Example: #NoHobbyists

```
hashtag#NoHobbyists when it comes to Cyber Security!  We cannot allow our people and our teams to be completely self-taught in their free time!   We all agree to shift security left, but is that realistic?

Security is a shared sport. We all sense a shortage of "cyber pros" and we are placing tremendous faith in automated pipelines.

In addition to designing/implementing mission logic and the tests to visibly  demonstrate quality, developers are front and center cyber-security. Upskilling and innovative tools are not developer focused!

We must protect and enable developers and the entire stream aligned team! Here are ideas and techniques from my experience and from quite a few peers and luminaries:

++Put structure around developer education - Most security training for developers lacks depth and examples.  Focus on the 70:20:10 model with 70% on the job experience and challenges , 20% informal like mentoring and 10% formal training.

I'm doing this now with stream-aligned teams and avoiding heavy upfront training.  This doesn't mean no formal training; it means focus on applying what has been learned. Gary Gruver's EDT and the new DEVOPS INSTITUTE pathways take this approach!

++Teach developers to think about breaking things -Sounds counterintuitive? Nope!  Developer mental maps are to create new and build.  Include "how to break it" dialog in training, visioning, peer reviews and paired walkthroughs.

I advocate a short tour-of-duty with true operations folks who are frontline in daily debugging.

Give the entire delivery team time to play with the OWASP training projects like WebGoat. https://lnkd.in/dHzVk_aq

++Plan time and funding for Upskilling and Education - Perhaps the biggest challenge to reducing hobbyists is to commit funding and plan time for cornerstone learning.

++Treat Thread Modeling as a Team sport - Build a shared culture by threat modeling together.  Here is a quick cheat sheet from my favs, OWASP: https://lnkd.in/dXcMHyus

++Encourage Experimentation - This is the basis for all modern software engineering!  With experimentation will come outcomes. Whether you label them success or failure, learn fast and iterate, celebrating each new outcome. Bryan Finster and I focus on this daily!

++Embrace Shared Responsibility And Give Grace - Cybersecurity is shared responsibility. Band together to move past our decades of retribution fears. See Lisa Plaggemier's Security Optimism 2022 https://lnkd.in/dphB2T_z

Synopsys has a quick post on security training for devs: https://lnkd.in/db3c47h4

If you take away a single thought, it is that we cannot lob more responsibility onto developers without arming them, and the organization!!  Security is not for self-taught hobbyists.

#devops #cybersecurity #humansfirst #devsecops #threatmodeling #ST4G
```

## Pattern 4: Empathetic/Analogical (~300-350 words)

Extended metaphor sustained throughout, grace-focused.

### Example: Continuous Exhaustion (Swimming Analogy)

```
**Continuous everything is leading to continuous exhaustion.** The root cause will surprise you:  good intentions!    If we want to encourage growth and improvement, we need to start with patience, grace, and understanding of our people, teams, and organizations.

To understand why we are exhausted, let's use the analogy of learning to swim.  Everyone has a different affinity for water and swimming.  Everyone's physical condition and stamina differs.

Some are naturals and jump into the deepest coldest water with seeming little impact.  They bob to the surface and say "come on in!"  Not all fall into that category.

+Value over velocity - Many in our software architecture and engineering field have been pushing back hard at the over-focus on velocity.  "GO FAST" is not the goal.  We need to deliver value at the speed of relevance.

Teach everyone about value: what it is, how to measure, and what is their role in delivering business/mission value.

+Be ready with swimmies, kickboards, and  life-rings.  Some of your team will need just a slight confidence boost that comes with arm-swimmies.  Others will have confidence and will need practice that comes with a kickboard.  Occasionally, toss a life-ring and keep you people afloat. hashtag#HumansFirst

+Pepper in laser-focused teaching moments - When someone is struggling to make that first length of the pool, give them one improvement to get them to the end:
 "Keep breathing on every stroke", "kick from the hips", "remember to pull".

A simple reminder on one aspect can often give the small improvement needed to finish that length.

+Encouragement always - I cannot overstate the importance of sincere encouragement!  This is such a contagious act.  Clap, cheer, laugh, smile.  We are in this together.

+Rest and catching your breath is important - Sometimes, you need to hold onto the side of the pool and catch your breath.  Give yourself and your people that respect.  And once they've caught their breath, encourage them to try again.

+Make the lifeguard visible always - It is important to everyone that they know someone is there to bail them out, to buffer them, to protect them, and sometimes, to call them out when they go astray.

Every organization and every team and every person should know the basic lifesaving skills.  How can we tell if someone is in trouble? What should we do based on our own skill level.   The Red Cross used to teach new lifeguards to triage "reach, through, row, go".

Ultimately, this impacts quality, morale, cyber security, resiliency, and quiet honestly, every dimension and aspect of organization. Addressing this keeps hashtag#HumanDebt and hashtag#techdebt at bay.

I'd love to hear how you are helping your teams, organizations and yourself to be continuous without being exhausted.

#software #team #devops #devsecops #learning #softwarearchitecture #ST4G #CommunityofCommunities
#inthistogether #CICD #valuestream #wellness
#cyberresiliency #cyber #cybersecurity
```
